Gerald "Sarge" A. Marking

Hall of Fame Wrestling Coach

Twenty Four Years of Wrestling Excellence

  • 1956 - Gerald " Sarge" Marking graduated from Baraboo High School
  • 1963 - Marking earned a Bachelor of Science Degree in Education at the University of Wisconsin-Platteville and served in the US Army
  • 1966 - "Sarge" started the wrestling program at Campbellsport High School. Initially, he took the position for one year. Marking had never wrestled and had virtually no knowledge of the sport. Marking's first season had a 4-5 record
  • 1967 - Present - From 1967 to the present, coached his teams to a winning season each year and Cambellport Wrestling compiled a dual meet record of 259-21-1.
  • 1970-1978 - Cambellsport set a record with 101 consecutive dual meet wins in the Flyway Conference.
  • Team - His teams have won 23 conference titles. Marking's teams earned 146 consecutive conference wins, 11 undefeated seasons and 14 undefeated seasons at home, with a home record of 129-7-0. In the 24 years of wrestling, Campbellsport shut out 38 teams. They won 13 WIAA Regional and 6 WIAA Sectional Championships. In 1980, Marking's team won the WIAA Class B State Championships and in 1979, his team placed 2nd.
  • Individuals - In his 24 years of coaching: He coached 159 individual conference champions, 59 of Marking's wrestler swere WIAA state qualifiers, 23 became place winners. Nine of his wrestlers made it to the WIAA State Finals and 3 became Wisconsin State Champions.
  • 1995 - Sarge retired from Campbellsport Wrestling

" His love of wrestling as a molder of young men are well known among those familiar with him. He has always handled his athletes with firm but fatherly discipline and always given a boy a push when a push is needed and a hug when that was called for. He is a fierce and fiery competitor and he does a remarkable job of instilling the same competitiveness in his wrestlers.""

--Steve Engelland, wrestling coach at Neenah High School
